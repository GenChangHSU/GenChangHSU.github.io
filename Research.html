Areas of Interests

I have broad interests in ecology, be it theoretical or empirical, pure or applied. In particular, I am most interested in the following research topics:

  • Climate change ecology: geographical/phenological shifts and synchrony/asynchrony between interacting species, multiple stressors and ecosystem responses

  • Trophic ecology: multi-trophic interactions and feedback/feed-forward mechanisms, food web dynamics, intra-guild predation, non-consumptive effects of predators on prey, keystone predators and community regulation, stable isotope ecology

  • Species interactions: predator-prey/host-parasite eco-evolutionary dynamics, species competition, niche differentiation and coexistence, mutualistic relationships, above- and below-ground biotic interactions

  • Community ecology: community assembly processes, priority effects, dispersal limitation, environmental filtering, island biogeography

  • Agro-ecology: crop-pest-enemy dynamics and biological control, working landscapes and sustainable agriculture

Of course, I am not limiting myself to only these areas. For me, ecology, as an encompassing discipline, should be integrative and interactive rather than fragmented and isolated. With this philosophy in mind, I am always glad to work on any ecological questions that spark my interest!

I have over six years of experience in R programming, mainly relevant to statistical analysis as well as some ecological modeling. I am also interested in data wrangling and visualization: creating awesome ggplot2 figures brings me a great source of joy and achievement!
